Structure of the RGE and WGCOP

The YMCA-RGE is the environmental platform within the World Alliance of YMCA’s, which is the umbrella organization for all national YMCA-movements. Within the YMCA-RGE there are currently three active working groups, the Working Group on Environmental Initiatives (bridging environmental action of YMCAs from all over the world), the Working Group on Paris (hosting Camp Climate among other things), and the Working Group on the Conference of the Parties. The WGCOP is currently led by two WG-Leaders called Oscar Villacorta (Peru) and Petter Bjersér (Sweden) and is consisting of four WG-Teams, Activism, Policy, Communications and Finance.

  • The Activism Team is led by Karin Edstedt (Sweden) with the focus on creating effective actions online and onsite in Paris to raise the climate ambition. The Activism Team is then responsible for setting up the Activism Group which will attend COP21.
  • The Policy Team is led by Jail Ixel (Mexico) with the focus on keeping track of all matters related to the negotiations and all ways the YMCA can influence the negotiations through our own lobbying, policies & advocacy. The Policy Team is then responsible for setting up the Policy Group which will attend COP21.
  • The Communications Team is led by Johan Manner (Sweden) with the focus of communicating with the greater YMCA, international media and creating communications strategies on social media. The Communications Team is then responsible for setting up the Communications Group which will attend COP21.
  • The Finance Team is led by Eugene Mel (Cameroon) with focus of acquiring financing for WG-Members. This Team is the most important when it comes to making the YMCA-RGE into the global movement that the YMCA already is. To acquire financial resources is something that is vital to strive for equitable participation. This Team is not represented by a specific group in the conference, but members of this Team will have a high priority when it comes to receiving accreditations and improving the grand movement that we want to become.
